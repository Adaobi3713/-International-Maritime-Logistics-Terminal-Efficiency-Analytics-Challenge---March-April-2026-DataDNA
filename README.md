# -International-Maritime-Logistics-Terminal-Efficiency Dashboard  
### DataDNA Dataset Challenge – Onyx Data   

## 📌 Overview

This repository contains a data visualization dashboard created for the **DataDNA Dataset Challenge** hosted by **Onyx Data**. The project analyzes international maritime logistics data to uncover operational inefficiencies, with a specific focus on the impact of the **Suez Canal disruption** and terminal-level bottlenecks.

The dashboard moves beyond simple container counts to answer critical questions:
- How did cargo volume trends shift during the disruption period?
- Is there a measurable difference between **day and night** terminal operations?
- Which specific terminals are the biggest bottlenecks when comparing **volume vs. efficiency**?

## 🗺️ Dashboard Structure

### Page 1: Suez Disruption Impact
- **Cargo Volume Trend:** Visualizes volume fluctuations tied to the Suez Canal blockage timeline.
- **Day vs. Night Efficiency:** Compares average move duration to highlight operational slowdowns during night shifts.

### Page 2: Regional & Vessel Trends
- **Regional Hubs:** Movement duration trends broken down by major maritime regions.
- **Vessel Analysis:** Efficiency segmented by vessel category and build group age.

### Page 3: Terminal Bottleneck Analysis
- **Volume vs. Efficiency Scatter Plot:** Identifies terminals with high throughput but disproportionately long move durations.
- **Top 10 Bottleneck Terminals:** A ranked list of terminals requiring immediate operational attention.

## 💡 Key Insights

1. **Suez Impact:** The disruption caused visible volatility in cargo volume trends.
2. **Night Shift Lag:** Operations are on average **~11 hours slower** per move during night hours, suggesting potential for labor optimization or shift rescheduling.
3. **Hidden Bottlenecks:** Several high-volume terminals exhibit move durations well above the baseline capacity of **5,147 hours**, indicating critical congestion points that mask overall supply chain health.
